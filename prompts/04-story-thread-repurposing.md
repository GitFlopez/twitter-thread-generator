# Prompt 4: Story Thread + Full Repurposing Kit

**Format:** Narrative arc → lesson → soft CTA  
**Goal:** Connection, trust, personal brand, lead generation  
**Length:** 8-15 tweets  

---

## The Prompt

```
You are an expert Twitter/X thread writer specializing in personal story threads that build genuine connection.

Write a story thread AND a complete repurposing kit for the following:

**The story:** [STORY — describe the event, experience, or arc. Include: when it happened, what the situation was, what you tried, what failed, what the turning point was, and what you learned]
**The lesson/takeaway:** [LESSON — what should the reader walk away believing or doing differently?]
**Target audience:** [AUDIENCE — who will this resonate with most?]
**Your goal:** [GOAL — leads, followers, brand awareness, product awareness, emotional connection, job search?]
**Voice:** [VOICE — how personal do you want to get? Scale of 1-5: 1=professional/restrained, 5=raw/vulnerable]

---

**PART 1: Story Thread**

Write the story thread with the following structure:

**Tweet 1 (The Hook Moment):**
- Drop into the most dramatic or surprising moment of the story: "In [year/timeframe], I [dramatic event or decision]. I had no idea what was coming."
- OR: "[Number] months ago, I [situation]. Today, [outcome]. Here's the full story: 🧵"
- Creates a gap: "what happened?" — reader has to scroll to find out
- Must be under 280 characters
- Hook score: rate 1-10 and explain what emotion this creates

**Tweets 2 through [N-3] (The Arc):**
- Narrative arc in 3 phases:
  Phase 1 (Setup): Who were you? What was the situation? What were you trying to do?
  Phase 2 (Struggle): What went wrong? What did you try? What failed? What was the cost?
  Phase 3 (Turn): What changed? What insight, person, or event shifted everything?
- Write in past tense, first person
- One scene or moment per tweet
- Use sensory detail where possible: "It was 11 PM on a Tuesday" beats "it was late"
- Every tweet ≤280 characters

**Tweet [N-2] (The Lesson):**
- The explicit takeaway: "What I learned from all of this: [1-2 sentence insight]"
- Should feel earned — not generic advice, but something only someone who lived this story could say

**Tweet [N-1] (The Application):**
- Connect the lesson to the reader's situation: "If you're [in reader's situation right now], this is for you: [1 sentence of advice or encouragement]"

**Tweet [N] (The Close + CTA):**
- Give the reader somewhere to go
- Options: "Follow me for more stories like this" / "DM me if you're going through something similar" / "I wrote a [guide/thread/tool] about this — reply with [word] and I'll send it" / "What was your version of this moment? Reply below."
- Warm close — this is a relationship thread, not a sales thread

---

**PART 2: Repurposing Kit**

After the thread, generate all 4 repurposed versions:

**LinkedIn Post Version:**
- Condense the full thread into one LinkedIn post
- Open with the hook moment (same as Tweet 1 but expanded to 2-3 sentences)
- Tell the arc in 4-6 paragraphs (no tweet structure)
- End with the lesson + a question to the reader
- Optimal length: 1,200-1,800 characters
- Use line breaks generously (LinkedIn rewards scannable posts)
- Include 3-5 relevant hashtags at the end

**Email Newsletter Version:**
- Subject line (3 options: curiosity / benefit / story hook formats)
- Preview text (40-90 chars)
- Full narrative version of the story in email format
- More detail than the thread — emails allow 400-800 words
- Personal sign-off
- One clear CTA at the end (reply, click, share)

**Blog Post Outline:**
- SEO title (incorporating the lesson as a searchable phrase)
- H2 structure (6-8 sections based on the thread arc)
- Suggested intro hook
- Word count recommendation
- Internal link opportunities (3-4 spots where other content would fit)

**Instagram Carousel Script:**
- Slide 1 (Cover): Title that mirrors Tweet 1 hook
- Slides 2-8: Key moments from the story (one per slide, 15-25 word captions)
- Slide 9 (Lesson slide): The takeaway in bold, shareable format
- Slide 10 (CTA slide): Follow prompt + call to action
- Caption: 150-200 word narrative with hashtags

**Format rules:**
- Each tweet labeled "Tweet [N]:" with character count [247 chars]
- Flag tweets over 280 chars with ⚠️
- Plain text within tweets; markdown OK in repurposing kit sections
```

---

## When to Use This Format

- You have a personal experience with a clear lesson
- You want to build genuine trust with your audience
- You're job hunting and want to demonstrate judgment and character
- You're launching something and want to explain the "why I built this"
- You want to convert observers into followers or buyers

---

## Tips for a Great Story Thread

1. **The moment matters more than the lesson.** "I almost quit" is more compelling than "I learned about persistence." Start with the moment.
2. **The struggle makes it real.** If everything worked perfectly, it's not a story — it's a brag. Include what failed.
3. **The turn should feel surprising but inevitable.** The best turns make readers think "of course — I should have seen that coming."
4. **Keep each tweet one scene or one sentence.** Long tweets in story threads kill pacing.
5. **The repurposing kit is the ROI multiplier.** One story → 1 thread + 1 LinkedIn post + 1 email + 1 blog outline + 1 Instagram carousel = 5 pieces of content from one prompt.
