# Prompt 3: Contrarian Thread (Opinion + Debate)

**Format:** Provocative claim → argument → nuance → ask for debate  
**Goal:** Engagement (replies, quote tweets), audience growth, brand positioning  
**Length:** 6-10 tweets  

---

## The Prompt

```
You are an expert Twitter/X thread writer specializing in high-engagement contrarian opinion threads.

Write a contrarian thread for the following:

**Topic:** [TOPIC — what conventional wisdom are you challenging?]
**Your position:** [POSITION — what do you actually believe that most people in your field disagree with?]
**Your evidence/reasoning:** [EVIDENCE — why are you right? Data, personal experience, or logical argument]
**Target audience:** [AUDIENCE — who do you want to engage with this? Who should push back?]
**Your tone:** [TONE — are you confident/provocative, curious/exploratory, or analytical/measured?]
**What you're NOT saying:** [NUANCE — what's the caveat or exception to your position?]

---

Write a contrarian thread with the following structure:

**Tweet 1 (The Stake in the Ground):**
- State the contrarian position directly: "[Popular belief] is wrong." OR "Hot take: [your position]." OR "I'll say what [industry people] won't:"
- Must be designed to trigger a response — either strong agreement ("FINALLY someone said it") or strong disagreement ("this is insane")
- Do NOT hedge in tweet 1. The nuance comes later.
- Must be under 280 characters
- Hook score: rate 1-10 and explain what reaction this should trigger

**Tweets 2 through [N-2] (The Argument):**
- Build the case tweet by tweet
- Tweet 2: state the evidence or the core of your argument
- Middle tweets: supporting points, examples, data, or stories
- Use "Here's why:" or "The data says:" or "I've seen this firsthand:" to signal credibility
- Every tweet ≤280 characters
- Don't strawman — represent the opposing view fairly before tearing it down

**Tweet [N-1] (The Nuance/Caveat):**
- This is the maturity signal. Add the exception: "The one case where the conventional wisdom IS right: [narrow exception]"
- This prevents the thread from being dismissed as extreme
- Shows you've actually thought this through, not just being provocative for engagement

**Tweet [N] (The Ask for Debate):**
- Invite pushback directly: "Change my mind." OR "Where am I wrong?" OR "Who disagrees — I want the strongest counter-argument."
- Do NOT say "like and retweet" — ask for a substantive reply
- Consider: "If you agree, share this with someone who needs to hear it."

**After the thread, provide:**
- **Predicted reply patterns:** 3 types of replies this will likely get (agree, disagree, add nuance) and how to respond to each
- **Escalation risk:** Rate 1-5 how likely this is to blow up negatively, and what to watch for
- **Follow-up thread idea:** If this thread performs well, what's the natural next thread to write?

**Format rules:**
- Each tweet on its own line with "Tweet [N]:" label
- Character count in brackets: [247 chars]
- Flag tweets over 280 chars with ⚠️
- Plain text only within tweets
```

---

## When to Use This Format

- You have a genuine opinion that differs from the mainstream
- You want to be known for a specific point of view
- You're trying to attract a specific type of follower (the ones who agree with you)
- You want to position against a competitor or dominant methodology
- You have data or experience that contradicts common advice

---

## Tips for a Great Contrarian Thread

1. **You need to actually believe it.** Fake contrarianism gets exposed immediately. Your evidence must be real.
2. **The nuance tweet saves you.** "I agree this isn't universally true" preempts the most common criticism.
3. **Don't name names unless you're ready for a fight.** "Some frameworks are wrong" is safer than "[Named framework] is wrong."
4. **The best contrarian tweets have a villain.** Not a person — a belief, a system, or a status quo.
5. **Respond to the first 10 replies within 1 hour.** Engagement in the first hour determines if Twitter shows the thread to more people.
